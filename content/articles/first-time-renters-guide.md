## Budget: How Much Can You Afford?

### The 30% Rule

Most experts recommend spending **no more than 30% of your gross income** on rent.

| Annual Income | Monthly Gross | Max Rent (30%) |
|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| $30,000 | $2,500 | $750 |
| $40,000 | $3,333 | $1,000 |
| $50,000 | $4,167 | $1,250 |
| $60,000 | $5,000 | $1,500 |
| $75,000 | $6,250 | $1,875 |
| $100,000 | $8,333 | $2,500 |

### The Real Cost of Renting

Rent is just the beginning. Budget for:

| Expense | Typical Cost | Frequency |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Rent | Your budget | Monthly |
| Security deposit | 1-2 months rent | One-time |
| Utilities | $100-250/month | Monthly |
| Internet | $50-80/month | Monthly |
| Renter's insurance | $15-25/month | Monthly |
| Parking | $0-200/month | Monthly |
| Application fee | $25-75 | Per application |
| Moving costs | $200-2,000 | One-time |

**First month total:** Rent + deposit + fees + moving = 3-4x monthly rent

## Documents You'll Need

### Standard Requirements

| Document | What It Is | Where to Get It |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **ID** | Driver's license or passport | Your wallet |
| **Proof of income** | 2-3 recent pay stubs | Employer |
| **Bank statements** | 2-3 months | Your bank |
| **Tax returns** | Last 1-2 years | IRS or accountant |
| **References** | Personal or professional | People who know you |
| **Credit report** | Your credit history | Free annual report |

### If You're Self-Employed

- 2+ years of tax returns
- Profit/loss statements
- Client contracts or invoices
- Bank statements showing consistent income

### If You Have No Rental History

- Letter from parent or guardian
- Reference from employer
- Reference from professor (if student)
- Offer to pay larger deposit

## Apartment Hunting

### Where to Search

| Platform | Best For |
|----------|----------|
| Apartments.com | Large inventory |
| Zillow | Houses and apartments |
| Facebook Marketplace | Local, direct landlords |
| Craigslist | Budget options (watch for scams) |
| Local Facebook groups | Neighborhood-specific |
| Walking around | "For Rent" signs |

### Red Flags (Avoid These)

| Warning Sign | What It Might Mean |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Way below market price | Scam or major issues |
| No photos or generic photos | Hiding something |
| Landlord wants cash only | Tax evasion, not legitimate |
| Pressure to sign immediately | Desperate or hiding issues |
| Won't let you see unit | Scam |
| Asks for deposit before viewing | Definitely a scam |

### Questions to Ask

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| What's included in rent? | Utilities, parking, amenities |
| What's the lease term? | 6 months? 12 months? Month-to-month? |
| What's the pet policy? | Fees, restrictions, breed limits |
| How is maintenance handled? | Response time, emergency contact |
| What's the parking situation? | Included? Extra cost? Street only? |
| Who are the neighbors? | Noise, demographics |
| What's the move-in cost? | First + last + deposit + fees |

## The Walkthrough

### What to Check

**In every room:**
- [ ] Lights turn on/off
- [ ] Outlets work (bring a phone charger to test)
- [ ] Windows open and close
- [ ] Door locks work
- [ ] Smoke detector present
- [ ] No water damage on ceiling/walls

**In the kitchen:**
- [ ] Faucet works (hot and cold)
- [ ] Drain isn't clogged
- [ ] Garbage disposal works
- [ ] All appliances turn on
- [ ] Refrigerator is cold
- [ ] Cabinets are clean

**In the bathroom:**
- [ ] Toilet flushes properly
- [ ] Shower has good pressure
- [ ] Hot water works
- [ ] Fan works
- [ ] No mold or mildew
- [ ] Caulking is intact

**Overall:**
- [ ] No pest signs (droppings, traps)
- [ ] No strange smells
- [ ] Heating/AC works
- [ ] Hallways are clean
- [ ] Building feels safe

### Document Everything

**Take photos of:**
- Every room
- Any existing damage
- Appliances
- Meters
- Common areas

**Why:** Protects your security deposit later

## Understanding the Lease

### Key Terms to Understand

| Term | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **Lease term** | How long you're committed |
| **Security deposit** | Money held for damages |
| **Rent due date** | When payment is expected |
| **Grace period** | Days before late fee |
| **Late fee** | Charge for late payment |
| **Subletting** | Renting to someone else |
| **Guest policy** | How long visitors can stay |
| **Notice period** | Days before you can leave |

### Negotiable Items

Sometimes you can negotiate:

| Item | How to Ask |
|------|------------|
| Rent | "Is there flexibility on price for a 12-month lease?" |
| Move-in date | "Can I move in on the 15th and prorate?" |
| Pet fee | "Can we waive the pet deposit?" |
| Parking | "Is parking included?" |
| Lease term | "Is month-to-month available?" |

### Never Sign If...

- You haven't seen the actual unit
- You don't understand something
- The lease differs from what you were told
- You feel pressured
- Terms seem illegal or unfair

**Always read the entire lease. Every word.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I save before moving out?

**Minimum:** 3x monthly rent (first month + deposit + emergency fund)

**Recommended:** 4-5x monthly rent

### Do I need renter's insurance?

**Yes.** It's cheap ($15-25/month) and protects your stuff. Many landlords require it.

### Can a landlord reject me for having no credit?

Yes, but they must apply criteria consistently. Alternatives:
- Co-signer
- Larger deposit
- Proof of income
- References

### What if I need to break my lease?

Options:
- Sublet (if allowed)
- Pay early termination fee
- Negotiate with landlord
- Find replacement tenant

**Consequences of just leaving:** Lawsuit, collection, damaged credit

### Should I get a roommate?

Pros:
- Split costs
- Shared responsibilities
- Less lonely

Cons:
- Less privacy
- Potential conflicts
- Joint lease liability

**If you do:** Have a roommate agreement
